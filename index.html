John See is a Senior Lecturer with the Faculty of Computing and Informatics at Multimedia University, Malaysia. He is currently the Chair of the Centre for Visual Computing (CVC) and he leads the Visual Processing (ViPr) Lab. From 2018, he is also a Visiting Research Fellow at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU) as an award recipient of the Belt and Road Initiative Young Scientist Fellowship.

My research interests span the areas of computer vision and pattern recognition in general, and the emerging fields of affective computing and computational aesthetics. I am currently working on a broad spectrum of visual surveillance tasks: action and activity recognition, long-term video analytics and person/group re-identification. Recently, I have also shifted attention to study the computability of highly subjective tasks such as facial micro-expression analysis, aesthetics and styles of photographs, and emotion from images.

He is currently serving as an Associate Editor of the EURASIP Journal of Image and Video Processing (IF: 1.737) and IEEE Access (IF: 3.557). He also served as the Technical Program Chair of MMSP 2019, and is currently in the TPC for ACM Multimedia, ICIP, ICME. He is a Senior Member of IEEE.
